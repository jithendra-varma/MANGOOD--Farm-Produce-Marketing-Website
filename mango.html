India the largest producer and exporter of Mangoes

Mango (Mangifera indica Linn) is known to the world as a fruit with many nutritional qualities, as it is rich in minerals, fibre, vitamins and provitamins. Also referred to as the peach of the tropics, this fruit is one of the main types of fruit considered exotic. Because of it's sweet, refreshing and aromatic, it is commercialized throughout the world. Main producer of these mangoes is india.

It is the national fruit of India, Pakistan, and the Philippines, and the national tree of Bangladesh. Approximately 50% of all tropical fruits produced worldwide are mangoes. Mango is commercially grown in more than 80 countries. World production of mango for the year 2014 was more than 46 million tons (FAOSTAT 2016). Major Mango producing countries of the world are India, Brazil, China. Thailand, Egypt, Indonesia, Mexico, Pakistan, Philippines, and Vietnam.

By far, Asian countries produce the largest volumes of mangoes worldwide. India is the world's largest producer by a wide margin, with more than 40% of global production. India produced 18.77 million tons of mango of the total world mango production in 2014 (FAO-STAT 2016). China and Thailand stood at second and third position among mango producing countries in the world with 4.77 and 3.43 million tons respectively.

Mango Varieties

There are over a 1000 varieties of mangoes available in the market.
*The varieties list below are those which are produced in our village.

Juice Varieties

Alphonso

Available during May, this variety is called as Khadar in our village. It is most loved by people for their sunshine yellow appearance and delightful taste and is rightly known as the King of mangoes. Named after Afonso de Albuquerque, the voluptuous king of the mangoes. They are medium in size, ovate oblique in shape, orange yellow in colour. Mainly exported as fresh fruit. It has average price of 40Rs/Kg.

Sindhura

Available during April-May, this variety is called as Arrakaya(Red Fruit) in our village. Sindhura mango is soft and juicy fleshed fruit having an distinct appearance with shades of green and red. It is also known as Honey Mango because of its exceptional flavour and sweetness. The term Sindhura came about due to its apperance with pleasing reds and greens among the golden skin, that resembles Sindoor. It has an average price of 20Rs/Kg.

Totapuri

Available during May-June, this variety is called as Bengalora in our village. It gets its name because of its parrot beak shape and green colour with red tinges. Totapuri mangoes are eaten raw with some salt and chilly powder. They are sour and sweet and has a unique flavour. Totapuri mangoes are used to prepare pickles, salads and mango juices. It is the most popular mango variety for making pulp. It has an average price of 10Rs/Kg.

Neelam

Available during July-August, marking the end of mango season. Neelam Mangoes are known for their beautiful shape, acidic-sweet taste and divine floral aroma. Neelam Mangoes have a specific sweet smell and are usually smaller with the mangoes being a bit on the orange side of colour. It has an average price of 20Rs/Kg

Powder Varieties

Rumani

Rumani mango are round in shape ressembling the size of large oranges. They have very thin skin. They are known for sour taste. Due to this sour taste they are used as alternative for Tamarind. Rumani mangoes are main mangoes used for preparing amchoor powder i.e., dried mango powder. Which is used as an mango flavouring agent in soups and dishes. It has an average price of 15Rs/Kg.

Eatable Varieties

Banginapalli

Banganpali also called as safeda in north or Benishan in our village is a popular fruit in several regions in Andhra Pradesh. Often called as The King of Mangoes in South India, this fruit is significantly larger than other varieties of mangoes commonly sold mid-season in the market and on average weighs about 350 - 400 grams. Exhibiting meaty texture, the thin and firm skin of this mango is sweet in taste and lacks fibre. It has an average price of 25Rs/Kg.

Shakkarakatti

Also called as chakkarigotti is a variety of eatable mango. Which is very small in size about the size of a lemon. It is the smallest mango also called as Baby mangoes. It is a sweet fibrous fruit. It is so fibrous to eat that to eat it you have to chew it about 10 minutes to feel its taste. It has an average price of 25Rs/Kg.

Premium Varieties

Imam Pasand

Also famously known as Himayat is one of the most tasty mangoes in India. The mango is big in size with a very small, thin seed, ensuring maximum pulp in the fruit. Himayat/Imam Pasand is considered as the Royal mango out of all Mango varities. People who once tasted Himayat mango can never be so excited about any other mango variety, because of the sweet aroma and unmatched taste. It is the costliest mango being produced sold in our village costing over 100Rs/Kg.

Malgoa

Malgoa also called as malgoba in our village. It is a large round fruit (typically 300-500 g), it has a small hard seed inside and is very juicy and fragrant. It is generally considered to be one of the best mangoes. The Malgova is a round shaped mango which retains a green colour (with hints of red) when ripe. It is roundish-oblique, in shape, with a blunt apex and has a smaller lateral beak than other mangoes. For the size of fruit, the stone is small. The fiber-less flesh is yellow, soft, and has a flavor that is rich, spicy, and sweet. It would cost around 40Rs/Kg.
